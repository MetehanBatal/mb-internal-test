The One Thing That Helped Me Sleep During Menopause

Tuesday, October 1
Linda Evans

If you’re reading this, chances are, you’ve had the same type of sleepless nights as I have. Rolling around, sweating, staring at the clock just waiting for sleep to come. But so often during menopause, it just doesn’t.

I thought I knew what was coming. Perimenopause was an eye-opening introduction to how much sleep can be affected by changing hormone levels. But did I know it was going to be this bad? Not even close.

The biggest factor in my sleepless nights was those terrible hot flashes. All of a sudden, that heat wave would go through my whole body and I’d be sweaty and instantly awake. It got so bad, I had a fan set up directly in front of my face to cool me down, no matter the weather. That’s on top of running the air conditioning all the time (you don’t even want to see my electric bill).

Something had to change.

I scoured the internet, read books, and asked my friends for advice. I started exercising more to tire me out for bed and cut off coffee at 1 pm. Those small changes helped, but here’s what consistently, majorly helped my sleep.
